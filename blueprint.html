Overview

‍     This is a personal project that my fellow classmates and I decided to work on. This project consists of trying to create a more efficient way to plan classes at UCSD. We chose to embark on this project because we understand how stressful it can be to create this plan, and we understand that a combination of resources can be used to make this an easier process.

     To discover what students prioritize and need when planning classes, we sent out 2 surveys, receiving responses from over 100 students, with 37 different majors. We also interviewed 15 students to gain a deeper understanding.

Current Interface

Based on the survey, we asked some students to send us screenshots of their current academic plans, with all of them using Google Sheets or Excel as their platform. The layouts ranged from very detailed and complex plans, to simple lists of required classes, since there is no standard template.

plans.ucsd.edu

This website that generates what classes you need to take, and when you should take them. This can be very useful, but many students wish that it was more customizable since it doesn't take into consideration things like adding a minor or implementing credits received from other institutions.

College Requirements

At UCSD, each student is placed into one of the 6 different colleges within the university, with each college having different general education requirements.

Major Requirements

Similar to the colleges each major typically has its own website where all of the courses are listed, typically separated by lower division, and upper division. Everything is very spread out, and one student even complained that since it's spread out, it's hard to keep track of everything you need to get done.

Next Steps

     In order to gain more data and help from administration, we are planning to meet with our design professor, Michael Meyer. We want to also see things from an administrative stand point to help bridge the communication between students and advisors. We're also looking to get feedback and guidance on this project since we would like to advocate for this program to be implemented.
